How You Can Help

Volunteers are the heart of our operation at Barb's Dog Rescue. Whether you live locally or are visiting Rocky Point, there are many ways you can help make a difference:

  • Walk dogs and provide much-needed socialization
  • Help with feeding, cleaning, and basic care
  • Assist with adoption events and fundraisers
  • Transport dogs to vet appointments or adoption events
  • Help with administrative tasks and social media

No experience is necessary! We'll train you in all aspects of dog care and rescue operations. Whether you can volunteer for a few hours, a day, or on a regular schedule, we welcome your help and support.

If you're visiting Rocky Point, spending time at the rescue can be a rewarding part of your vacation. Many of our regular volunteers started as visitors who fell in love with our mission.

FAQ

Volunteer Questions

Do I need to speak Spanish to volunteer?

No, while some Spanish is helpful, it's not required. Many of our staff members speak English, and we have bilingual volunteers who can assist with translation when needed.

Can I volunteer if I'm only visiting Rocky Point for a short time?

Absolutely! Many of our volunteers are visitors who spend a few hours or days helping during their vacation. Even a short visit makes a big difference to our dogs.

Is there a minimum age requirement for volunteers?

Volunteers under 18 must be accompanied by an adult. Children as young as 8 can volunteer with parental supervision, helping with tasks appropriate to their age and abilities.

What should I wear when volunteering?

Wear comfortable clothes that you don't mind getting dirty. Closed-toe shoes are required. We recommend bringing a hat, sunscreen, and water bottle, especially during hot months.
